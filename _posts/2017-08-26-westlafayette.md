My experience with food thus far in West Lafayette has been pretty interesting. As a first year freshman, I find myself running around campus or desperately trying to navigate the swarms of people on my knock off penny board during my cross campus trips to various classes or to study at a library with friends. So in all the hubbub and frenzy of syllabus week, I decided to seek the comfort of the more familiar notion of the Purdue Farmer‚Äôs Market.

So apparently in the Midwest it starts getting cold in the middle of August! In California, the heat builds up all the way to the end of August and bursts into a shower of falling leaves that leaves a few trees bare for winter. Here the cold has already started to sink in, with the average temperature not reaching above 80! It‚Äôs a big shock for a California kid like me who spent two months in a tropical country over the summer. I‚Äôm usually still in shorts and a t shirt at this time of the year, but in Indiana I‚Äôm forced to wear jeans, closed toe shoes and a light sweatshirt in order to stay warm! So how I found this little spot is simple: I saw a long-ass line and decided to get in! I figured Indiana must be pretty good at barbecue since they‚Äôre a midwestern state (you know, just generic Californian stereotypes about the world) and I must say I‚Äôm mildly disappointed. I waited less than ten minutes before I got to the smoky grill where I tall, middle-aged cook manned the grill, tending to a handful of patties and sausages before turning his attention to the smoker. They served them in the little rectangular paper bowls, and I watched as the server piled one rib after another into the bowl, probably totaling to like six ribs! I paid with cash then walked down to the stand for condiments where I got a good helping of honey mustard and good old barbecue sauce. I walked back to the bench my friend Noah was sitting at and that‚Äôs where my day took a turn.

The first rib I bit into was alright ‚Äì actually I have to say that it was really fucking dry. I hope I‚Äôm not wrong when I say that ribs are supposed to be quite moist and juicy, with the meat falling off the bones. Instead I bit through what I guess was a mix between protein and some sand. It was definitely very tough meat, and the first thought that ran through my head was ‚Äúwow, I bought some really expensive jerky that‚Äôs still on the bone.‚Äù The next few ribs were sort of the same, with the sauces more or less saving the day.
